Germination is a game about building a combo of stomps on dangerous flora on a tiny planet; it's a lot like Downwell mixed with Super Mario Galaxy.

I wrote Germination in C++, using SFML for graphics and FMOD Studio for music and custom sound spatialization (more below).
It released to positive reviews on Steam, where it makes use of the leaderboards, achievements, and friend network.

The music has a set of layers, each with its own variety of behavior. Most are linked to the number of a certain type of enemy onstage at a time.
As a result, the layer might adjust not only its volume but also generate completely different notes (or choose to generate more or fewer altogether). In the trailer, for example, the distinct spooky theramin sound relates to the red floating plant enemies that hover.

Because of the shape of the game world (literally a circle), choosing how to position sounds in the world was important.
I implemented a special position parameter in the sound events that handle it: When sounds are on the other side of the planet (>90 degrees difference), they're positioned in "real space" and are additionally filtered so that they sound occluded.
But when a gameplay sound happens in view of the player, it needs to happen in stereo, so that the player can think of it in terms of the game plane. Additionally, the most dangerous enemies and projectiles in the game have distinct alert sounds that are modulated based on relevant gameplay aspects, like their distance from the player.

Responsibilities: I handled the programming, sounds/foley, music, and writing. Cadence Petersen drew all of the pixel graphics.
