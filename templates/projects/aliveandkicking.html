Portal: Alive & Kicking is a total conversion of Portal 2 to remaster the orignal game.
In 2017, Valve Software gave us access to the source code so that the game could reach its full potential.

As both a sound designer and sole programmer, I put extra care into creating new sound engine features that would give the game a distinct identity.

These features were used both by myself and by level designers adding game sounds to levels.
Most of these features were implemented as a result of feedback from the rest of the team, or to alleviate playtester suggestions.

One of the more unique features I implemented was a loop point modulator, which allows the loop cues of a sound to change dynamically, creating a sort of granular synthesis effect.
Building the loop modulator feature required modifying the Source Engine's sound engine on every level, from the sound operator scripting language all the way down to the lowest-level, oldest parts of the game engine.

In-game, playtesters enjoyed the new music on bouncing energy balls, but were annoyed that they couldn't tell how long one would last before expiring.
With the new feature, I gave the energy balls an electronic glitch effect that would increase in intensity just before the ball expires.

I also used the game's Squirrel scripting language and state machines combined with sound scripting to automate the music score based on the player's interaction with the mechanics.
In some maps, the music swells as the player flings themselves into the air through a portal, while energy balls, cubes, and the portals themselves all hum along, their ambient gameplay sounds tuned to the soundtrack.

In others, the game uses a state machine to track which layers should sound as the player moves through a dangerous turret level.
A separate layer exists for the base level, the secret behind the scenes area, danger, and for defeating the turrets.

Responsibilities: I handled the programming, sounds, music, and some level design, especially for problem areas that needed scripting or IO help.
I also wrote descriptions for the Steam store page and produced in-world "investment videos" to play on the elevators at the beginning and end of levels.

The music and sounds were produced in Ableton Live.
