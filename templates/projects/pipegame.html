Pipegame is a prototype puzzle game I made for an Interactive Music in Games course in my second year.
It was designed to teach players some basic concepts of music theory.
Players must guide the flow of colored fluids through pitched pipes to the exit under the constraints of the puzzle.

I wrote Pipegame in Unreal Engine 4, using both Blueprints and C++.
It integrates FMOD Studio for all of the sound.

All of the game events are triggered through callbacks in the sound engine.
For example, a series of puzzle elements (like the pipes filling), is all linked to a musical timeline such that they fit in a single measure of music.
This allows for the player to solve the puzzle on their own time, but the solutions will always be executed musically.
